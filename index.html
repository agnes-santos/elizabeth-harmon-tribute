Elizabeth Harmon

Elizabeth "Beth" Harmon is a chess prodigy and the main character in the Netflix miniseries, The Queen's Gambit.

Information below contains spoilers and are sourced from the Beth Harmon page at The Queen's Gambit Wiki.

November 2, 1948

Elizabeth Harmon was born to Alice and Paul Harmon

1953

Her father officially gave up on trying to see her since Alice had kept "running away". Harmon realizes that her mother had taken the same vitamins, Librium

July 25th, 1957

Beth is sent to Methuen Home, an exclusively-female Christian orphanage, after her mother died in a traffic collision.

She meets Jolene and became close friends through mutual admiration and understanding of each other.

1957

Beth began learning chess after noticing orphanage's custodian, Mr. Shaibel, playing alone, whereby she became fascinated with the checkered pattern of the eight-by-eight grid.

Mr. Shaibel later on brings Mr. Ganz, the head of two local chess clubs, into the basement to face-off against Harmon. After she defeats both of them after several games, even simultaneously, Mr. Ganz photographs Beth next to Mr. Shaibel to show her off to the local chess club.

Beth is invited by Mr. Ganz to play against the local chess team and bests the 12 players in a little over 80 minutes

1963

Beth is adopted by the Wheatley family and plays her first tournament after asking Mr. Shaibel to invest in her entrance fee.

She went on to win in the Cincinnati, Pittsburgh and Houston Tournaments

1966

Beth loses to Benny Watts in the US Championship and is dubbed the US Co-Champion.

She befriends her Russian language classmates and experienced being a teenager attending the party.

Beth graduated from Fairfield High School and received a Bulova gift from her mother.

She loses against Vasily Borgov in Mexico City Invitational Chess Tournament, and her mother dies in questionable causes. Harry helps her cope with grief through attempting to teach her chess but eventually parted.

1967

Beth wins against Benny in the Ohio US Championship after identifying further weaknesses in her play and train together for her international career growth in New York.

In the Paris Remy-Vallon Invitational, Harmon is prepared to face off against Borgov once more, but she is held back by relapsing the night before. She gradually spirals into her addictions and even fleding before the Kentucy State Championship tournament, forfeiting her title.

1968

Jolene appears on Beth's doorstep to attend Mr. Shaibel's funeral. Both talk about their lives and future through adult lenses. Jolene finances Beth's trip to Moscow out of her own pocket.

Beth wins against seven Russian Grandmasters in the Moscow Invitational. She leaves to play chess in a public park.
